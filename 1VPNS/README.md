<h1>OFAC Designation:</h1>

<h2>Dmytro Rashevskyi and First VPN Service / 1VPNS</h2>

<p>
  On July 13, 2026, the U.S. Department of the Treasury’s Office of Foreign Assets Control added cryptocurrency addresses associated with <strong>Dmytro Rashevskyi</strong>, administrator of <strong>First VPN Service / 1VPNS</strong>, and with 1VPNS itself.
</p>

<p>
  Treasury states that 1VPNS sold infrastructure to ransomware operators and other cybercriminals, including groups targeting U.S. businesses, hospitals, financial institutions, and municipal governments.
</p>

<h2>Why This Matters</h2>

<p>
  These are not merely incident-adjacent wallets. They are officially blocked identifiers associated with Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons.
</p>

<p>
  Treasury states that the associated infrastructure enabled:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>Ransomware deployment</li>
  <li>Concealment of attacker origin and network activity</li>
  <li>Management of exfiltrated data</li>
  <li>Cybercriminal access to infrastructure used against public- and private-sector targets</li>
</ul>

<blockquote>
  Any direct or indirect exposure should be treated as both an attribution signal and a sanctions-risk signal.
</blockquote>
