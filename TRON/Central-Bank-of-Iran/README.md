<h1>OFAC Designation Update</h1>

<h2>Central Bank of Iran — TRON Address Expansion</h2>

<p>
  On July 14, 2026, the U.S. Department of the Treasury’s Office of Foreign Assets Control materially expanded the cryptocurrency attribution associated with the <strong>Central Bank of Iran</strong> by adding four TRON addresses to its existing Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons entry.
</p>

<p>
  The Central Bank of Iran entry is linked by OFAC to the <strong>Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps-Qods Force</strong> and <strong>Hizballah</strong> under Iran-related and counterterrorism sanctions authorities.

<h2>Why This Matters</h2>

<p>
  This is a material attribution expansion, not a repost of the earlier Central Bank of Iran designation.
</p>

<p>
  The four newly added wallets are now official blocked identifiers associated with Iran’s central bank and its stated links to the IRGC-Qods Force and Hizballah. They should be ingested as direct sanctions-watch seeds.
</p>

<p>
  Direct counterparties, exchange deposit addresses, intermediary wallets, bridges, and other connected infrastructure may be useful graph-expansion pivots. However, transactional proximity alone does not make those counterparties sanctioned or prove that they are controlled by the Central Bank of Iran.
</p>
